About Me

I'm a PhD researcher at the Royal College of Surgeons Ireland, investigating non-coding driver mutations in breast cancer. My work combines computational biology, machine learning, and genomics to uncover novel regulatory mechanisms that could lead to new therapeutic approaches.

With a First Class Honours degree in Genetics and Cell Biology from Dublin City University, I've built expertise in integrating large-scale genomic datasets (TCGA, ENCODE, DepMap) with cutting-edge machine learning frameworks to address critical questions in precision oncology.

Beyond research, I'm passionate about science communication and have been recognized for translating complex genomic research into accessible insights for diverse audiences.
