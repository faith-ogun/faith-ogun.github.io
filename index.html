About Me

Building the bridge between code and cure.

I'm a first-year PhD student at the Royal College of Surgeons Ireland, investigating non-coding driver mutations in breast cancer using large-scale genomic datasets including TCGA, ICGC, and ENCODE.

My research applies machine learning and integrative computational approaches to identify functional variants in regulatory elements, aiming to uncover novel therapeutic vulnerabilities in breast cancer subtypes.

I completed my B.Sc. in Genetics and Cell Biology at Dublin City University with First Class Honours, ranking 2nd in my cohort and earning a place on the Dean's Honours List.

Featured Project

OncoSynth

Multi-Agent System for Synthetic Lethality Research

OncoSynth is an intelligent multi-agent framework that automates synthetic lethality research for cancer therapeutics. Built with CrewAI and powered by ChatGPT models, the system orchestrates specialized AI agents to perform literature mining, druggability assessment, and clinical trial analysis.

The framework processes biomarker-target gene pairs through a coordinated pipeline, generating comprehensive research reports with statistical scoring and therapeutic recommendations.
