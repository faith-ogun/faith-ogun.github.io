ABOUT ME

I am a first-year Ph.D. student at the Royal College of Surgeons Ireland, investigating non-coding driver mutations in breast cancer using large-scale genomic datasets including TCGA, ICGC, and ENCODE. My research applies machine learning and integrative computational approaches to identify functional variants in regulatory elements, aiming to uncover novel therapeutic vulnerabilities in breast cancer subtypes. I completed my B.Sc. in Genetics and Cell Biology at Dublin City University with First Class Honours, ranking 2nd in my cohort and earning a place on the Dean's Honours List.

I have expertise in Python, R, and bioinformatics tools, with hands-on experience in neural networks (PyTorch, scikit-learn, XGBoost) and cloud-based AI systems (Google Cloud Platform, Vertex AI). My work has been funded by the Government of Ireland Postgraduate Scholarship Programme, a nationally competitive award with an 18% success rate. I was awarded 2nd place at the 2025 Google Ireland Hackathon and received the Best Lay Presentation award at Breakthrough Cancer Research.

I am passionate about applying computational methods and machine learning to solve real-world problems in precision oncology, developing scalable bioinformatics pipelines, and building AI-driven tools for biomarker discovery.

HIGHLIGHTED PROJECT

OncoSynth

Multi-Agent System for Synthetic Lethality Research

OncoSynth is an intelligent multi-agent framework that automates synthetic lethality research for cancer therapeutics. Built with CrewAI and powered by ChatGPT models, the system orchestrates specialized AI agents to perform literature mining, druggability assessment, and clinical trial analysis. The framework processes biomarker-target gene pairs through a coordinated pipeline, generating comprehensive research reports with statistical scoring and therapeutic recommendations.

The system features both interactive CLI and batch processing modes, enabling high-throughput analysis of gene pairs from DepMap CRISPR screens and TCGA data. OncoSynth demonstrates advanced prompt engineering, agent orchestration, and scalable workflow automation—key skills for developing production-ready AI systems in biotech and pharmaceutical research.
